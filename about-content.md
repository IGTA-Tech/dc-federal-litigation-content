# About DC Federal Litigation Counsel

*Federal court representation from Washington, DC*

---

## Our Mission

DC Federal Litigation Counsel exists to provide access to federal courts for clients who need experienced representation in the nation's capital. We focus exclusively on federal court litigation and administrative law matters that require counsel admitted to practice in DC federal courts.

Our federal court admissions allow us to represent clients in matters that many attorneys cannot handle—from pro hac vice sponsorship to complex litigation before the Court of Federal Claims and DC Circuit Court of Appeals.

---

## Why Federal Court Admission Matters

Many attorneys are admitted only to state courts or district courts outside Washington, DC. When litigation arises in DC federal courts, these attorneys need local counsel or pro hac vice sponsorship to participate.

**Our federal court admissions include:**

- U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia
- U.S. Court of Federal Claims
- U.S. Court of Appeals for the DC Circuit
- U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit
- Supreme Court of the United States

These admissions allow us to:
- Serve as local counsel for out-of-jurisdiction attorneys
- Sponsor pro hac vice applications
- File cases directly in federal court
- Appear at hearings and argue motions
- Handle appeals to the circuit courts

## Our Practice Areas

### Federal Court Services

**DC Local Counsel & Pro Hac Vice Sponsorship**
We serve as local counsel and sponsor pro hac vice applications for attorneys from other jurisdictions appearing in DC federal courts.

**Federal Court Ad Hoc Counsel**
We provide limited-scope representation for specific court appearances when lead counsel cannot attend.

### Immigration Federal Court Litigation

**USCIS Mandamus Lawsuits**
We sue USCIS in federal court to compel action on unreasonably delayed immigration applications.

**Visa Denial Appeals**
We challenge arbitrary and capricious USCIS decisions through APA litigation in federal court.

### Court of Federal Claims Practice

**Tucker Act Claims**
We pursue monetary claims against the federal government for contract breach, takings, and statutory violations.

**Bid Protests**
We represent contractors challenging federal procurement decisions at both COFC and GAO.

### Government Contracts

**SBA Certifications**
We assist with 8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB, and WOSB certification applications and appeals.

**Suspension & Debarment Defense**
We defend contractors facing exclusion from federal contracting.

### Regulatory Litigation

**EPA & Environmental Litigation**
We challenge EPA actions and defend against environmental enforcement in DC Circuit and district court.

**FOIA Requests & Litigation**
We obtain government records through FOIA requests and federal court litigation when agencies refuse disclosure.

## Our Approach

### Focused Practice

We concentrate on areas where federal court litigation provides advantages over administrative processes. Rather than waiting years for agency appeals, federal court litigation often resolves matters in months with independent judicial review.

### Efficient Representation

We structure engagements to provide value. For routine matters like pro hac vice sponsorship, we offer flat fees. For complex litigation, we provide detailed cost estimates and regular communication about case progress.

### Nationwide Reach

Federal courts in Washington, DC have nationwide jurisdiction over many matters. The Court of Federal Claims hears cases from plaintiffs across the country. DC District Court is a preferred venue for immigration mandamus and APA challenges. Our DC admissions allow us to serve clients regardless of their location.

---

## Why Washington, DC?

Washington, DC is the center of federal government litigation. Federal agencies are headquartered here, making DC the natural venue for challenging agency actions.

**DC advantages:**
- EPA, USCIS, and other agencies headquartered in DC
- DC Circuit has exclusive jurisdiction over many regulatory challenges
- Court of Federal Claims has nationwide jurisdiction
- Concentrated judicial expertise in administrative law
- Efficient case resolution compared to other districts
